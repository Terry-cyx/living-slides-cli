# Copywriting Formulas

The difference between "topic" and "insight" in slide titles.

## The Cardinal Rule

**Slide titles should state the insight, not the topic.**

| ❌ Topic | ✅ Insight |
|---------|-----------|
| "Q3 Revenue" | "Revenue doubled in Q3" |
| "Our Architecture" | "One API call replaces 12 services" |
| "Customer Feedback" | "93% of users renewed after first month" |
| "Pricing" | "Starting at $9, no credit card required" |
| "Team" | "40 engineers who built YouTube and Stripe" |
| "Market Size" | "$42B market growing 23% annually" |
| "Competitive Landscape" | "Only we combine real-time + offline" |
| "Problem Statement" | "Every presentation takes 8 hours to make" |

**Test**: Does the title give away the punchline? If yes, good. If it just describes what's on the slide, rewrite.

## Core Formulas

### PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
**Use**: Problem slides, pain-point setup
**Structure**: Problem → Agitate → Solution
**Template**: "[Pain point]? Every [time frame], [consequence]. [Solution] fixes this."

**Example**:
> "Creating a deck takes 8 hours. Every week, your team loses a full day to slide design. living-slides cuts that to 30 minutes."

### AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
**Use**: CTAs, closing slides, opening hooks
**Structure**: Attention → Interest → Desire → Action
**Template**: "[Bold statement]. [Benefit detail]. [Social proof]. [CTA]."

**Example**:
> "PowerPoint is dying. AI can't generate it, your team hates making it, and no one can version-control it. 10,000 teams switched to living-slides last quarter. Try it free →"

### FAB (Features-Advantages-Benefits)
**Use**: Feature slides, product descriptions
**Structure**: Feature → Advantage → Benefit
**Template**: "[Feature] lets you [advantage], so you can [benefit]."

**Example**:
> "Visual editor + AI changelog integration lets you refine slides without losing your edits, so you can iterate 5x faster than manual revisions."

### Cost of Inaction
**Use**: Urgency, agitation slides
**Structure**: Status quo → Loss → Time decay
**Template**: "Without [solution], you're losing [amount] every [timeframe]."

**Example**:
> "Without living-slides, your marketing team wastes 30 hours/month formatting slides. That's $45,000/year in lost productivity."

### Before-After-Bridge
**Use**: Case studies, transformation stories
**Structure**: Before → After → Bridge
**Template**: "Before: [pain state]. After: [desired state]. How: [your solution]."

**Example**:
> "Before: 3 designers spent 2 weeks per quarterly deck. After: 1 person ships it in 4 hours. How: AI generation + visual editing + intelligent refinement."

### Value Stack
**Use**: Pricing slides
**Structure**: List everything included with dollar values, total, then your price
**Template**: "Feature A ($X) + Feature B ($Y) + Feature C ($Z) = Total $XYZ. Your price: $N."

## Headline Patterns

### Power Words
- "Stop [bad thing]"
- "Get [result] in [timeframe]"
- "The [adjective] way to [action]"
- "Why [audience] choose [product]"
- "[Number] ways to [goal]"
- "From [pain] to [benefit]"

### Contrast
- "[Old way] is dead. Meet [new way]."
- "Don't [bad]. Instead, [good]."
- "Most [category] do X. We do Y."

### Social Proof
- "[Number]+ [companies] trust [product]"
- "Join [notable] and [notable]"
- "Featured in [publication]"

### Curiosity
- "The [surprising thing] about [topic]"
- "What [unexpected source] taught us about [topic]"
- "[Unusual number] things we learned from [experience]"

## Body Copy Rules

1. **Sentences over fragments** — "42% growth" < "Revenue grew 42% year-over-year"
2. **Active voice** — "We shipped 3 features" not "3 features were shipped"
3. **Concrete over abstract** — "$2.4M" not "significant revenue"
4. **Present tense for current state** — "10,000 users trust us" not "10,000 users trusted us"
5. **Cut filler words** — kill: "very", "really", "just", "actually", "basically"
6. **Numbers beat adjectives** — "94% accuracy" not "highly accurate"

## Formula by Slide Type

| Slide Type | Primary Formula | Target Emotion |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------|
| Opening hook | AIDA, Curiosity | curiosity |
| Problem | PAS, Agitate | frustration |
| Cost/Risk | Cost of Inaction | fear |
| Solution reveal | FAB, BAB | hope |
| Feature dive | FAB | confidence |
| Traction proof | Proof Stack | trust |
| Testimonial | Testimonial quote | trust |
| Pricing | Value Stack | confidence |
| Closing CTA | AIDA, Urgency | urgency |

## Translation for Chinese Decks

Chinese slide titles also follow the insight rule:

| ❌ 话题式 | ✅ 洞察式 |
|---------|---------|
| "Q3 业绩" | "收入同比增长 42%，创历史新高" |
| "产品特性" | "一次点击，替代整个工作流" |
| "市场机会" | "420 亿美元市场，年增长 23%" |
| "团队介绍" | "来自 Google、字节的 40 位工程师" |

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ "Agenda" / "议程" — show the content, not the outline
- ❌ "Thank You" as the final slide — end with CTA or next action
- ❌ "Any Questions?" — skip it, people will ask
- ❌ "About Us" as early slide — tell them what you'll do for them first
- ❌ Subheadings that repeat the title
- ❌ Bullet points that don't parallel ("Improve" + "Performance is optimized" + "Better user experience")
- ❌ Starting sentences with the same word repeatedly
- ❌ Generic buzzwords: "cutting-edge", "best-in-class", "synergy", "leverage"

## The 10-Word Rule

If your slide has more than 10 words of content (excluding title), you're probably saying too much. Split into multiple slides, or replace text with visuals.

## Final Check

Before finalizing any slide, read just the titles in sequence. Do they tell a coherent story? If your audience only read the titles, would they get the point?
