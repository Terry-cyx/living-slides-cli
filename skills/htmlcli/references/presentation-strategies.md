# Presentation Strategies

Proven deck structures and emotion arcs. Pick one based on context.

## Strategy Selection Matrix

| Context | Use Strategy | Slides |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Raise seed funding | YC Seed Deck | 10-12 |
| Raise Series A | Series A | 12-15 |
| Sell to prospects | Sales Pitch | 7-10 |
| Product demo | Product Demo | 5-8 |
| Board update | Board Meeting | 15-20 |
| Quarterly review | QBR | 10-15 |
| Team all-hands | All-Hands | 8-12 |
| Conference talk | Conference | 15-25 |
| Training session | Workshop | 20-40 |
| Case study | Case Study | 8-12 |
| Executive briefing | Executive | 5-8 |

## YC Seed Deck (10 slides)

**Goal**: Raise seed funding
**Audience**: Early-stage VCs, angels
**Emotion arc**: curiosity → frustration → hope → confidence → trust → urgency

1. **Title/Hook** — Company name, one-line description, logo
2. **Problem** — Pain point (use PAS formula)
3. **Solution** — What you built (use FAB formula)
4. **Traction** — Big numbers showing growth (use Big Number layout)
5. **Market** — TAM/SAM/SOM
6. **Product** — Screenshots or demo
7. **Business Model** — How you make money
8. **Team** — Why you can execute
9. **Financials** — Revenue projection (line chart)
10. **The Ask** — Amount raising, use of funds, CTA

## Series A Deck (14 slides)

1. Title/Hook
2. Company overview (1-liner)
3. Problem
4. Solution
5. Product (2-3 slides of screenshots)
6. Traction (metrics + growth chart)
7. Market size
8. Business model
9. Go-to-market strategy
10. Competition
11. Team
12. Financials (3-year projection)
13. Use of funds
14. Contact/Ask

## Sales Pitch (9 slides)

**Goal**: Close a deal
**Audience**: Qualified leads
**Emotion arc**: connection → frustration → fear → hope → trust → confidence → urgency

1. **Personalized Hook** — Reference the prospect by name/company
2. **Their Problem** — Show you understand their specific pain
3. **Cost of Inaction** — What they lose by not acting
4. **Your Solution** — How you solve it
5. **Proof/Case Studies** — Similar customers' results
6. **Differentiators** — Why you vs. competitors
7. **Pricing/ROI** — Investment and return
8. **Objection Handling** — Pre-empt common concerns
9. **CTA + Next Steps** — Clear action

## Product Demo (6 slides)

1. Hook / Problem statement
2. Solution overview (1 sentence)
3. Demo screenshots or video
4. Key features (3 max)
5. Benefits / ROI
6. CTA

**Rule**: Demo should dominate — 50%+ of presentation time is the product walkthrough.

## QBR — Quarterly Business Review (12 slides)

**Goal**: Update internal leadership
**Audience**: Executives, team leads

1. Title + period ("Q1 2026 Review")
2. Executive summary (3 bullets max)
3. KPI Dashboard (4 metric cards)
4. Revenue trend chart
5. Wins (3-4 highlights)
6. Challenges (honest 2-3)
7. Customer metrics (NPS, churn, retention)
8. Product updates
9. Team updates
10. Budget / spend
11. Q2 priorities
12. Questions / discussion

**Rule**: Balance wins with honest challenges. Execs smell sandbagging.

## Conference Talk (18 slides)

**Goal**: Share insights, build thought leadership
**Audience**: Conference attendees

1. Title + speaker intro
2. Hook — "Have you ever...?"
3. Personal story / credibility
4. Main thesis
5-15. Three sections of 3-4 slides each (evidence, examples, stories)
16. Key takeaways (3 numbered)
17. Call to action
18. Contact / Q&A

**Pattern**: Use Duarte's sparkline — alternate "What Is" (pain) with "What Could Be" (hope) to create engagement peaks at 1/3 and 2/3 marks.

## Workshop / Training (30 slides)

Structure around exercises:

1-3. Intro, agenda, learning objectives
4-6. Concept 1 + example
7. Exercise 1
8-10. Concept 2 + example
11. Exercise 2
...continue...
Last 3. Recap, resources, next steps

**Rule**: 1 exercise per 6-10 slides. Never more than 15 minutes of lecture without interaction.

## Case Study (10 slides)

1. Title — "How [Customer] achieved [result]"
2. Customer background
3. Their challenge (PAS)
4. Why they chose you
5. Implementation approach
6. Results — big numbers
7. Testimonial quote
8. Lessons learned
9. How this applies to prospect
10. CTA

## Board Meeting (18 slides)

1. Title + period
2. Executive summary (1 slide)
3-6. Financial results (revenue, spend, runway, unit economics)
7-10. Operational metrics (growth, retention, NPS)
11-13. Strategic initiatives status
14-15. Key decisions needed from board
16. Risks + mitigations
17. Next quarter priorities
18. Q&A prompt

## Emotion Arcs

Every great presentation has an emotional journey, not a flat data dump.

### The Hero's Journey (for pitches)
```
setup → inciting incident → rising tension → climax → resolution
curiosity → frustration → fear → hope → confidence → urgency
```

### The Sparkline (Duarte, for talks)
```
What Is / What Could Be / What Is / What Could Be / New Bliss
pain      hope            pain      hope            resolution
```
Alternation creates tension. Breaks at 1/3 and 2/3 create engagement peaks.

### The Proof Stack (for sales)
```
problem → cost → solution → proof → proof → proof → ask
```
Stacking three proof points (case studies, testimonials, metrics) overwhelms skepticism.

## Slide Count Guidelines

**Sweet spots by format**:
- Investor pitch: 10-12 slides (max 15)
- Sales pitch: 7-10 slides
- Product demo: 5-8 slides
- Team update: 8-15 slides
- Conference talk: 15-25 slides (45min talk)
- Workshop: 30+ slides with exercises

**Too few signals**: Lack of substance
**Too many signals**: Rambling, unfocused
**Sweet spot**: 1 slide per 1-2 minutes of speaking time

## Opening Slides

First 3 slides determine if the audience pays attention. They should:
1. **Title slide** — hook within 5 seconds
2. **Problem/context** — establish relevance
3. **Promise** — tell them what they'll get from staying

## Closing Slides

Last 2 slides determine what they remember. They should:
1. **Recap / takeaway** — 3 bullets max
2. **CTA / next step** — ONE clear action

**Never end with** "Thank You" or "Questions?" — those are verbal, not slides. End on your CTA or key insight.
